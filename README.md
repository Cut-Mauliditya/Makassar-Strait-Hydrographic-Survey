# Hydrographic-Survey-in-Makassar-Strait
This survey was conducted in the Makassar Strait in collaboration with the National Research and Innovation Agency (BRIN) to characterize the region's physical oceanographic structure, including water mass properties and current patterns. CTD and ADCP data were processed using the instruments' embedded software.

I used Surfer and MATLAB primarily for plotting and visualization refinement, as these tools offered more flexibility than the embedded software for adjusting colormaps, contour intervals, and vector scaling. My contribution focused on producing publication-ready figures to illustrate thermocline structure, water mass properties, and current patterns (including the Indonesian Throughflow).

### Results
SST was ± 30°C, decreasing steadily with depth to 2–3°C at 2,000 m and a thermocline was observed between ± 200-600 m. Salinity values were >34.8 ppm (0.0348 PSU) at depths of 100–250 m and below 500 m, while a lower salinity of 34.6 ppm (0.0346 PSU) occurred between ± 250-500 m, indicating two distinct water layers. Surface density ranged from 1,016–1,018 kg/m³, increasing consistently to 1,037 kg/m³ at the seabed. Dissolved oxygen concentration increased from 6.4 mg/L at the surface to a peak of 10.6 mg/L around 1,500 m, remaining elevated to the seabed.

Horizontal current data showed that the overall flow through the Makassar Strait was directed north to south, with a mean speed of 370 mm/s. This pattern is consistent with the Indonesian Throughflow (ARLINDO), which transports Pacific water masses into the Indian Ocean via the Makassar Strait.Vertical current modeling revealed that the dominant flow was directed toward the seabed, interspersed with several short current paths. The maximum recorded current velocity was 2,496 mm/s at a depth of 95 m along transect 2, while the minimum velocity was 21 mm/s, observed at various depths.

Plots included: 
- Thermocline and Water Column Stability Profiles
- Water Mass Properties
- Horizontal and Vertical Ocean Circulation Visualization
